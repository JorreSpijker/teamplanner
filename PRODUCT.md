# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Club secretaries and team coordinators at korfball clubs. They use this tool at a desk, once or twice a season, to compose team rosters before the competition starts. They know the sport and the Competitie 2.0 rules well. Technical skill varies — some are club volunteers with basic computer literacy.

## Product Purpose

A generic, purpose-built planning tool for Dutch korfball clubs to compose valid teams according to Competitie 2.0 guidelines. It covers the full workflow: import a player list, create teams across categories, validate them against the rules, and export a clean submission. Success means a coordinator can complete the full cycle in a single session without confusion.

## Brand Personality

Clear, reliable, efficient. The tool is neutral — no club-specific branding — so any korfball club can adopt it. The design should feel like well-made sporting administration software: purposeful, trustworthy, and free of noise.

## Anti-references

No hard anti-references. Avoid by default: loud sports-scoreboard chrome (cluttered, ad-heavy), trendy SaaS productivity aesthetics (Notion/Linear minimalism), and enterprise IT scaffolding (SharePoint-heavy, form-heavy grey boxes). The tool should feel made for this specific purpose, not repurposed from another category.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity before density** — the rule system is already complex; the interface must not add cognitive load on top of it.
2. **Unambiguous feedback** — validation state (valid/invalid team, age bandwidth, player count) must be readable at a glance without interpretation.
3. **Task-first flow** — the core workflow (import → assign → validate → export) should be followable without a tutorial.
4. **Neutral identity** — no club-specific branding; visually neutral enough that any club can use it without feeling it belongs to someone else.
5. **Dutch directness** — all copy in Dutch; design sensibility matches the direct, practical character of the sport and its volunteer culture.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. No specific known user needs beyond standard. Drag-and-drop interactions must have keyboard alternatives. Color must not be the sole carrier of validation state — valid/invalid always uses both color and a label.
